# Lockstep Additional Use Grant (the "Indie Grant")

Lockstep is **fair-source**, not OSI "open source." The source is public, but
production use is governed by the [Business Source License 1.1](./LICENSE).
This file is the human-readable companion to the `Additional Use Grant`
parameter in that license. If the two ever conflict, the `LICENSE` text
controls.

## The short version

- **Indies & small studios — free.** If your organization's total gross
  revenue is **under USD $1,000,000/year**, you may use Lockstep in
  production for free, forever, including with your own private storage.
- **Above $1M/year — commercial license required.** Contact
  jannik811@gmail.com.
- **Everyone** may always read, fork, modify, and self-host the code for
  **non-production** use (evaluation, development, testing, contributing).
- **Each released version** converts to **Apache 2.0** four years after its
  release. Old versions become fully open over time.

## Definitions

- **"Organization"** means you and all entities that control, are controlled
  by, or are under common control with you (your affiliates), counted
  together.
- **"Gross revenue"** means total worldwide revenue from all sources
  (game sales, services, funding that is recognized as revenue, etc.) over the
  most recent trailing 12-month period.
- **"Production use"** means using Lockstep to manage real project data for
  work you intend to ship or monetize — as opposed to evaluating, developing,
  or contributing to Lockstep itself.

## FAQ

**I'm a solo dev / hobbyist. Can I use it?** Yes — free under the Indie Grant.

**We're a studio doing $400k/year. Free?** Yes.

**We just crossed $1M. What now?** Reach out for a commercial license; nothing
breaks in the meantime while we sort it out.

**Why not MIT/Apache from day one?** To keep Lockstep sustainable and stop a
hyperscaler from reselling it as a service. The four-year conversion to Apache
2.0 means the community still gets fully-open software on a predictable clock.

**Is this "open source"?** No, and we don't call it that. It is
**source-available / fair-source**. We are deliberate about the wording.
